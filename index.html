  • 1,000 subscribers AND 4,000 public watch hours (in 12 months)
  • OR 1,000 subscribers AND 10 million public Shorts views (in 90 days)

Monetization Warning

YouTube's biggest policy hurdle for AI channels is "repetitive or reused content." You must demonstrate significant human input (unique commentary, strong narrative, creative editing) to avoid demonetization.

Phase 3: Growth & Legal Guardrails

Building a sustainable channel requires consistent effort and a clear understanding of the rules of the road.

Consistency is Key:

Regular uploads signal to the YouTube algorithm that your channel is active. Aim for a consistent schedule, even if it's just one video a week to start.

Batch Your Workflow:

Don't create one video at a time. Dedicate a block of time to create all your content for the week‚Äîwrite all scripts, then generate all images, then record all voiceovers. This is far more efficient.

Engage with Your Community:

Reply to comments, ask questions in your videos, and build a community around your topic. This increases viewer loyalty and watch time.

Commercial Use Rights:

As highlighted, this is critical. Playground AI grants you full ownership, which is ideal. Be cautious with platforms that only offer a non-exclusive license. Always read the terms of service.

Copyright Compliance:

This is the fastest way to kill your channel. NEVER use music, video clips, or images you don't have a clear commercial license for. Stick to sources like the YouTube Audio Library and AI tools where you have explicit commercial rights.

AI Disclosure:

YouTube now requires creators to disclose when content is significantly modified by AI. Be transparent. You can also add a disclaimer in your video descriptions (e.g., "Visuals and voiceover generated with AI tools, with significant creative direction and scripting by a human author.").
