Phase 2: The Content Factory (Your First Video)

This is the core of your business. Here is the repeatable process for creating endless videos. Let's make your very first one: "What If The Titanic Never Sank?"

The script is your foundation. Use AI to generate ideas and first drafts, but always act as the editor-in-chief. Your role is to add unique perspectives, ensure historical accuracy, and craft a compelling story.

Recommended Free Tools:

  • ChatGPT (Free Tier)
  • Google Gemini (Free Tier)
  • Microsoft Copilot

Script Prompt:

Use this prompt in your chosen AI writing tool to get started:

Act as a world-class history storyteller. You MUST ONLY output the script for a 60-second YouTube Short titled "What If The Titanic Never Sank?". The script must have a powerful hook, build dramatic tension, and reveal the shocking long-term consequences. Structure it in 5 scenes. End with a call-to-action to subscribe.

Key to Success:

Raw AI output can be flagged by YouTube as "low-value, templated content." You MUST refine, fact-check, and rewrite sections to add your unique analysis. This human touch is non-negotiable for monetization.

A clear, engaging voiceover is your connection to the audience. Robotic voices will kill your retention.

Recommended Free Tool:

CapCut (Web & Desktop): Offers a wide library of natural-sounding text-to-speech voices with a commercial license option. The free plan has daily limits (10-20 minutes), so plan your work in batches.

Pro Tip for Natural Voiceovers:

To make the AI voice sound more natural, break long sentences into shorter ones and use punctuation (commas, periods) to guide the pacing and intonation. Experiment with different voice styles and speeds offered within CapCut.

This is where you bring the script, visuals, and voiceover together into a polished final product. CapCut is a surprisingly powerful and completely free video editor.

Key Free Features in CapCut:

  • **Video Editing:** Trim/Split/Merge, Speed Adjustment, Keyframe Animation, Smooth Slow-Motion (Optical Flow), Chroma Key, Stabilization, Multi-track Timeline
  • **Audio/Voiceover:** Text-to-Speech (Diverse voices, commercial license), Music Library (with copyright note), Sound Effects Library, Volume/Pitch Adjustment, Noise Reduction
  • **AI Tools:** Auto Captions (High accuracy, customization), Background Removal, AI Upscaler (Image/Video)
  • **Export Options:** Resolution (480p, 720p, 1080p, 2K, 4K), Frame Rate (24, 25, 30, 50, 60fps), Format (MP4, MOV)
  • **Asset Library:** Free Fonts, Effects, Filters, Transitions

Workflow in CapCut:

  • Start a new project.
  • Upload Your Assets: Upload the voiceover MP3 and the images you generated.
  • Match Scenes: Drag your generated images to align with your voiceover segments on the timeline.
  • Add Your Voiceover: Place your generated voiceover track.
  • Add Music & Captions:
    • For background music, use the official YouTube Audio Library. It's guaranteed monetization-safe.
    • Enable auto-captions from CapCut's "Text" tab for accessibility and SEO.
  • Generate & Download: Export your finished MP4 video file. For most YouTube content, 1080p (1920x1080) is recommended.

Phase 3: The Money Machine (Monetization)

Monetization is more than just ads. A diversified income strategy is key to building a resilient, passive income stream. Start these from day one.

This is the most common monetization method. To qualify, you need to meet YouTube's requirements and, crucially, adhere to their content policies.

Eligibility Thresholds:

  • 1,000 subscribers AND 4,000 public watch hours (in 12 months)
  • OR 1,000 subscribers AND 10 million public Shorts views (in 90 days)

Monetization Warning

YouTube's biggest policy hurdle for AI channels is "repetitive or reused content." You must demonstrate significant human input (unique commentary, strong narrative, creative editing) to avoid demonetization. This is why human editing of AI-generated content (Phase 2 steps) is crucial.

Step 3.4: Affiliate Marketing (The Easiest Income)

You earn a commission when someone buys a product through your unique link.

  • Sign up for Amazon Associates. It's free and easy.
  • **Find Relevant Products:** Search on Amazon for a best-selling, highly-rated book about your video topic.
  • **Get Your Link:** On the Amazon Associates dashboard, create your unique affiliate link for that product.
  • **Create a Kit.co Page:** This is a free and clean way to list all the books/products you recommend. Create a kit like "The What If? Historian Library" and add your product to it.

Phase 4: Launch & Growth (The First Month)

Building a sustainable channel requires consistent effort and a clear understanding of the rules of the road.

Upload Your First Video: Upload the "Titanic" video you created in Phase 2. Fill in the title and the description template (from Step 3.3). Add relevant tags like `what if`, `history`, `alternate history`, `titanic story`.

Create a Content Schedule:

Your goal is to post 3-5 Shorts per week.

Batch Create:

Don't make one video at a time. Dedicate one block of time (e.g., 3 hours on a Sunday) to create ALL your videos for the week. Follow the workflow in Phase 2.

Engage:

In the first few hours after posting, respond to every comment. This boosts your video in the algorithm.

Commercial Use Rights:

As highlighted, this is critical. Playground AI grants you full ownership, which is ideal. Be cautious with platforms that only offer a non-exclusive license. Always read the terms of service.

Copyright Compliance:

This is the fastest way to kill your channel. NEVER use music, video clips, or images you don't have a clear commercial license for. Stick to sources like the YouTube Audio Library and AI tools where you have explicit commercial rights.

AI Disclosure:

YouTube now requires creators to disclose when content is significantly modified by AI. Be transparent. You can also add a disclaimer in your video descriptions (e.g., "Visuals and voiceover generated with AI tools, with significant creative direction and scripting by a human author.").
